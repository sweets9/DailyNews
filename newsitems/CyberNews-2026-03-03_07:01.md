**Supply Chain Attacks Now the Most Common Threat to Businesses**
A global study by Kaspersky reveals that supply chain attacks were the most frequent cyber threat faced by businesses in the last year. Nearly one-third of all companies reported experiencing an attack that originated from a supplier or partner, with large enterprises being the most common targets. These attacks exploit trust and interconnectedness to infiltrate organizations.
Why it Matters: Your organization's security is not just about your own defenses; it's also dependent on the security of your vendors and partners. A single weak link in your supply chain can lead to a major breach, disrupting operations and compromising sensitive data. This requires a shift towards more rigorous third-party risk management.
Link: https://www.kaspersky.com/blog/supply-chain-threats-report/

**UK's Cyber Essentials Plus Certification Gets Major Overhaul**
The UK's widely adopted Cyber Essentials Plus (CE+) scheme is being updated in April 2026. The new version will require organizations to provide stronger technical proof that their security controls are effective. Key changes include mandatory multi-factor authentication (MFA) for cloud services, stricter timelines for applying security patches, and the inclusion of cloud and identity configurations in audits.
Why it Matters: For businesses operating in the UK or working with UK-based companies, these changes will raise the bar for security compliance. The move from documenting controls to proving their effectiveness means organizations will need more robust, measurable, and operational security practices to achieve or maintain certification.
Link: https://blog.qualys.com/product-tech/2024/03/05/cyber-essentials-plus-2026

**Advisory Warns of Coordinated Cyber Threats to Middle East Enterprises**
A security advisory has been issued highlighting persistent and coordinated cyber threat campaigns targeting critical infrastructure, industrial environments, and large enterprises in the Middle East. The advisory notes that attackers are increasingly focused on disrupting operations, not just stealing data, and are targeting both IT and Operational Technology (OT) systems.
Why it Matters: Organizations with operations or interests in the Middle East face a heightened and sophisticated threat environment. The focus on operational disruption poses a direct risk to business continuity, requiring a security strategy that unifies IT and OT defenses and prioritizes resilience against state-aligned threat actors.

**The Importance of Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT)**
Multiple security resources are highlighting the foundational role of Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT) in a modern enterprise security strategy. VAPT is a process that combines automated scanning to find vulnerabilities with manual testing (ethical hacking) to exploit them. This dual approach provides a realistic view of an organization's risk exposure.
Why it Matters: Understanding your weaknesses is the first step to building a strong defense. VAPT helps businesses move beyond a theoretical security posture to see how they would stand up against a real-world attack, allowing them to prioritize patching and strengthen controls where it matters most.
