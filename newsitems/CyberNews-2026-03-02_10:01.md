**BeyondTrust Flaw Actively Exploited to Breach Networks**
A critical vulnerability in BeyondTrust's remote support software is being actively used by hackers to gain initial access to corporate networks. The flaw, identified as CVE-2026-1731, allows an attacker to execute malicious code without needing to log in. Once exploited, attackers have been observed deploying backdoors to maintain long-term access, steal data, and disrupt services. The U.S. government has added this vulnerability to its "Known Exploited Vulnerabilities" catalog, requiring federal agencies to patch immediately.

Why it Matters: Remote support tools are a common entry point for attackers. A flaw like this gives hackers a direct path into a company's network, bypassing traditional security. The business impact includes potential for significant data breaches, financial loss, and operational downtime. Any company using this software is at immediate risk.

**Cisco Discloses Critical Zero-Day in SD-WAN Products**
Cisco has revealed a critical, unpatched "zero-day" vulnerability in its Catalyst SD-WAN Manager products. The vulnerability, tracked as CVE-2026-20127, has a maximum severity score of 10 out of 10. It allows an unauthenticated attacker to completely bypass security and gain full administrative control over the device. The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has issued an emergency directive, ordering federal agencies to apply mitigations immediately.

Why it Matters: SD-WAN is a technology used by many large organizations to manage their network traffic. A flaw of this severity could allow an attacker to intercept or reroute sensitive corporate data, listen in on communications, or move deeper into the network. Because it's a "zero-day," no official patch was available at the time of announcement, increasing the risk.
Link: https://www.esentire.com/security-advisories/cisco-catalyst-sd-wan-manager-vulnerability

**Critical Flaw in Juniper Routers Allows Full Device Takeover**
Juniper Networks has patched a critical vulnerability in its Junos OS Evolved software, which runs on their high-end PTX Series routers. The flaw, CVE-2026-21902, could allow a remote attacker without any credentials to execute code with the highest privileges, effectively taking complete control of the router. The issue is due to an incorrect permission setting that is exposed to the internet by default.

Why it Matters: Routers are the backbone of the internet and corporate networks. A compromised router can be used to redirect traffic, steal sensitive information, or launch attacks on other systems. The fact that this vulnerability is exploitable over the internet without authentication makes it extremely dangerous for any organization using the affected Juniper products.
Link: https://www.csa.gov.sg/alerts-advisories/alerts/al-2026-022
