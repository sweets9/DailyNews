**Enterprise Zero-Days Hit Record High in 2025, Google Reports**
Google's annual threat report reveals that attackers are increasingly targeting enterprise-specific technologies. Of the 90 zero-day vulnerabilities tracked in 2025, a record 43 were in enterprise software and appliances. The report highlights that security and networking products like routers and switches are prime targets. For the first time, commercial surveillance vendors (CSVs) were responsible for more zero-day exploits than state-sponsored groups.
Why it Matters: This indicates a major shift in the threat landscape. The focus on enterprise infrastructure means a single vulnerability can give an attacker broad access. The rise of CSVs makes powerful hacking tools more accessible to a wider range of threat actors.

**The Growing Risk of Insecure Messaging in the Enterprise**
Security experts are highlighting the dangers of using unauthorized or insecure messaging applications for business communications, a phenomenon known as "Shadow IT." These platforms often lack proper security controls, creating risks of data leaks, phishing attacks, and non-compliance with data protection regulations.
Why it Matters: Sensitive business information can be easily compromised through insecure messaging apps. Implementing a secure, centrally-managed enterprise messaging solution is crucial for protecting intellectual property and customer data.

**Network Security is a Core Business Requirement**
As businesses of all sizes continue their digital transformation, the importance of robust network security is greater than ever. Common threats include data breaches, ransomware, and insider threats. A comprehensive network security strategy is not just an IT issue but a fundamental aspect of business continuity.
Why it Matters: A security breach can lead to significant financial loss, reputational damage, and operational downtime. Proactive network security is essential for protecting a company's assets and ensuring its long-term viability.
