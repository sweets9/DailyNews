**Critical Vulnerability in n8n Workflow Automation Platform**

A critical vulnerability, nicknamed "Ni8mare," has been discovered in the n8n workflow automation platform, which is used by many businesses to connect different applications and services. This flaw could allow attackers to take over a company's n8n server, potentially leading to data theft and further attacks on their network. The vulnerability has the highest possible severity rating (10 out of 10). Patches are available, but tens of thousands of systems may still be at risk.

Why it Matters: An attacker could exploit this vulnerability to gain complete control over a company's automated workflows, which could include sensitive data and access to other critical systems. This could lead to significant data breaches and operational disruptions.

Link: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/ni8mare-flaw-in-n8n-platform-lets-hackers-hijack-servers/

**Russian State-Sponsored Hackers Target Europe and Central Asia**

The Russian-linked hacking group APT28 (also known as Fancy Bear) is conducting a large-scale campaign to steal login credentials from organizations in Turkey, Europe, and Central Asia. The group is using phishing emails that trick users into revealing their usernames and passwords.

Why it Matters: This is a classic espionage campaign aimed at stealing sensitive information from government, defense, and other high-value organizations. A successful attack could give the hackers access to confidential government and corporate data.

Link: https://securityaffairs.com/157291/apt/apt28-credential-harvesting-attacks.html

**Cyber-Fraud and AI Threats Now a Bigger Concern Than Ransomware for Business Leaders**

A new report from the World Economic Forum (WEF) shows that business leaders are now more worried about cyber-fraud and threats related to Artificial Intelligence (AI) than they are about ransomware. The report highlights the growing risks from AI-powered scams, deepfake videos used for impersonation, and phishing attacks.

Why it Matters: This shift in concern reflects the changing nature of cyber threats. While ransomware is still a major problem, businesses are increasingly facing a barrage of more frequent, smaller-scale attacks that can be just as damaging in aggregate. The rise of AI is making these attacks more sophisticated and harder to detect.

Link: https://www.computerweekly.com/news/366565158/Cyber-fraud-and-AI-risks-now-outpace-ransomware-concerns-says-WEF

**Zero-Click Attack Steals Data from ChatGPT Users**

A new attack has been discovered that can steal data from users of the popular AI chatbot, ChatGPT, without the user having to click on anything. The details of the attack are still emerging, but it appears to be a serious threat to the confidentiality of conversations and data shared with the chatbot.

Why it Matters: A zero-click attack is particularly dangerous because it requires no interaction from the user, making it very difficult to defend against. This could expose sensitive personal and business information that users have shared with ChatGPT.

Link: https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news/zero-click-attack-data-chatgpt/

**Personal and Health Data of 700,000 Illinois Residents Exposed**

The Illinois Department of Human Services (IDHS) accidentally exposed the personal and health information of nearly 700,000 residents. The data was left unprotected due to incorrect privacy settings.

Why it Matters: This is a major breach of sensitive personal information that could lead to identity theft and other forms of fraud. It highlights the importance of proper data handling and security configurations, especially for government agencies that hold large amounts of citizen data.

Link: https://securityaffairs.com/157262/data-breach/illinois-department-of-human-services-data-exposure.html

**17.5 Million Instagram Users' Data Leaked**

A large database containing the information of 17.5 million Instagram users has been leaked online. The data includes usernames, and other profile information. Meta, Instagram's parent company, has denied that the data came from a breach of their systems, and suggested it was from a password reset issue.

Why it Matters: Even if this data did not come from a direct hack of Instagram, it can still be used by attackers for phishing, spam, and other malicious purposes. It's a reminder to be cautious about the information you share online.

Link: https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/12/meta_instagram_password_leak/

**Telegram Flaw Exposes Users' Real IP Addresses**

A vulnerability has been found in the popular messaging app Telegram that can reveal a user's real IP address, even if they are using a proxy to hide it. This flaw affects both the Android and iOS versions of the app.

Why it Matters: An IP address can be used to track a user's location and online activity. For people who rely on services like Telegram for privacy and security, this is a serious flaw that could put them at risk.

Link: https://thehackernews.com/2026/01/12/one-click-telegram-flaw-exposes-real-ip.html

**CISA Adds More Actively Exploited Vulnerabilities to "Must-Patch" List**

The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has added several new vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog. This list includes security flaws that are known to be actively exploited by hackers. Federal agencies are required to patch these vulnerabilities within a specific timeframe.

Why it Matters: The KEV catalog is a critical resource for all organizations, not just government agencies. It provides a clear list of the most urgent security flaws that need to be fixed to prevent attacks.

Link: https://securityaffairs.com/157280/cisa/cisa-kev-catalog-hpe-oneview-ms-office-powerpoint-flaws.html

**North Korean Hackers Using QR Codes in Phishing Attacks**

The FBI is warning that hackers linked to North Korea are using QR codes in their phishing campaigns. The attackers send emails with QR codes that, when scanned, lead to malicious websites that steal login credentials.

Why it Matters: This is a new and clever tactic that takes advantage of people's familiarity with QR codes. It's a good reminder to be cautious about scanning QR codes from unknown or untrustworthy sources.

Link: https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/12/qr_code_phishing_north_korea/

**Misconfigured Email Settings Leave Microsoft 365 Users Open to Phishing**

Attackers are taking advantage of misconfigured email routing settings in Microsoft 365 to launch effective phishing campaigns. By exploiting these settings, attackers can make their phishing emails appear more legitimate and bypass some security filters.

Why it Matters: This highlights the importance of not only using strong passwords and multi-factor authentication, but also ensuring that all of your security settings are properly configured. Even a small misconfiguration can create a big security hole.

Link: https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news/misconfigured-email-routing-phishing/
