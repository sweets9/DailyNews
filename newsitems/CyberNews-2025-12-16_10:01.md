**Massive "React2Shell" Campaign Compromises Over 59,000 Servers**

A widespread and rapidly moving attack campaign dubbed "React2Shell" has compromised over 59,000 servers globally in a short period. The attacks exploit a critical remote code execution (RCE) vulnerability in applications built with the popular React and Next.js frameworks. The campaign is leveraging this vulnerability to deploy malware, including the "ZnDoor" and "PCPcat" strains, and to conduct further malicious activities. The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has issued an emergency directive for federal agencies to patch the vulnerability, highlighting the severity of the threat.

Why it Matters: The sheer scale and speed of this campaign make it a critical threat. The vulnerability is easy to exploit and affects a widely used development framework, putting a vast number of web applications at risk of complete takeover. Businesses using React or Next.js for their web applications should immediately investigate and apply the necessary patches to avoid being compromised.

Link: https://thehackernews.com/2025/12/massive-react2shell-campaign.html

**Google Issues Emergency Update for Actively Exploited Chrome Zero-Day**

Google has released an emergency security update for its Chrome web browser to patch a high-severity zero-day vulnerability. The vulnerability, tracked as CVE-2025-466192044, is a use-after-free flaw in a core component of the browser and is being actively exploited in the wild. Successful exploitation could allow an attacker to execute arbitrary code on a victim's computer, potentially leading to a full system compromise.

Why it Matters: A zero-day in a ubiquitous browser like Chrome is a serious threat. It allows attackers to target a massive user base with drive-by-download attacks and other malicious web-based techniques. All Chrome users should update their browsers to the latest version immediately to protect themselves from this threat.

Link: https://duocircle.com/cybersecurity-news/google-chrome-zero-day-december-2025

**Microsoft's December Patch Tuesday Fixes Actively Exploited Zero-Day**

Microsoft's final Patch Tuesday of 2025 addressed 56 vulnerabilities, including one zero-day that is being actively exploited. The zero-day, CVE-2025-62221, is a privilege escalation vulnerability in the Windows Cloud Files minifilter driver. An attacker who has already gained a foothold on a system can exploit this flaw to gain SYSTEM-level privileges, effectively taking full control of the machine. The update also includes fixes for three critical remote code execution vulnerabilities.

Why it Matters: While not a remote code execution bug itself, this zero-day is a valuable tool for attackers looking to escalate their privileges after an initial compromise. This makes it a critical vulnerability to patch. The other critical vulnerabilities in the update also pose a significant risk and should be patched promptly.

Link: https://duocircle.com/cybersecurity-news/microsoft-patch-tuesday-december-2025

**CISA Adds Two New Vulnerabilities to its "Must-Patch" List**

The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has added two new vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog. The vulnerabilities are CVE-2025-14611, a hard-coded cryptographic key in Gladinet CentreStack and Triofox, and CVE-2025-43529, a use-after-free vulnerability in Apple's WebKit. Both vulnerabilities are known to be actively exploited by attackers and pose a significant risk to federal and private sector organizations.

Why it Matters: The KEV catalog serves as a "must-patch" list for federal agencies and a strong recommendation for all organizations. The inclusion of these vulnerabilities indicates that they are being actively used in real-world attacks. Organizations using the affected products should prioritize patching to reduce their exposure to these known threats.

Link: https://www.cisa.gov/known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog

**700Credit Discloses Data Breach Impacting Auto Dealership Customers**

700Credit, a major provider of credit reporting and compliance solutions for the automotive industry, has disclosed a data breach that has exposed the personal information of a significant number of consumers. The exposed data includes names, addresses, and Social Security numbers. The company has not yet disclosed the number of individuals affected or the cause of the breach.

Why it Matters: This breach highlights the significant supply chain risk in the automotive industry. A compromise at a single vendor can expose the sensitive data of customers from thousands of dealerships. Affected individuals are at risk of identity theft and other forms of fraud.

Link: https://cyberpress.org/2025/12/16/700credit-data-breach/

**New Malware Strains Target Android and macOS Users**

Two new malware strains have emerged, targeting users of Android and macOS. "Frogblight" is a new Android banking trojan that is being distributed through fake government portals. The malware is designed to steal SMS messages and device information, likely to bypass two-factor authentication. On the Apple side, a new information-stealing malware called "MioLab MacOS" is being advertised on underground forums. The malware is capable of stealing a wide range of sensitive information from infected Macs.

Why it Matters: The emergence of new malware strains highlights the continuous evolution of the threat landscape. The "Frogblight" malware is a reminder of the dangers of downloading apps from unofficial sources, while the "MioLab MacOS" infostealer shows that macOS is not immune to malware.
