# Cyber Security News - 2025-12-26 10:01

*Timeframe: Last 24 hours*

**Critical Vulnerability in HPE OneView**
A critical remote code execution (RCE) vulnerability, identified as CVE-2025-37164, has been discovered in HPE OneView. The flaw scores a perfect 10.0 on the CVSS severity scale, indicating maximum risk. It allows an unauthenticated attacker on the same network to gain complete control over the management platform and any connected infrastructure. A publicly available proof-of-concept exploit module for the Metasploit framework makes attacks much easier to carry out.
Why it Matters: HPE OneView is used to manage servers, storage, and networking in enterprise data centers. A compromise could allow an attacker to take over fundamental IT infrastructure, leading to a complete network breach, widespread ransomware deployment, or catastrophic data loss. Immediate patching is critical.
Link: https://qsolit.com/critical-rce-in-hpe-oneview-cve-2025-37164/

**2025's Most Significant Vulnerabilities**
A review of 2025's security landscape highlights several major vulnerabilities that impacted enterprises. "React2Shell" (CVE-2025-55182, CVE-2025-66478) was a critical flaw in modern web frameworks like Next.js that allowed for remote code execution. A zero-day vulnerability in Ivanti's widely used Connect Secure VPN (CVE-2025-0282) was exploited by attackers before a patch was ready. Additionally, the ransomware group Storm-1175 used another flaw, CVE-2025-10035, to gain initial access to corporate networks before stealing data and encrypting systems.
Why it Matters: This trend shows that vulnerabilities in common, business-critical software (web frameworks, VPNs) remain a primary entry point for attackers. The exploitation of zero-days (flaws unknown to the vendor) demonstrates the speed and sophistication of modern cybercrime operations.
Link: https://socradar.io/the-top-10-cves-of-2025-a-year-in-vulnerabilities/

**Cybersecurity Trends to Watch in 2026**
Looking ahead, enterprise security is shifting focus. Instead of just scanning for vulnerabilities, leading organizations are adopting Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM), a more holistic approach that prioritizes threats based on their business context. Other major trends include the dual-edged sword of Artificial Intelligence, which is being used by both attackers and defenders, and the growing need to prepare for quantum computing's ability to break current encryption standards.
Why it Matters: The way cybersecurity is managed is changing. Businesses can no longer rely on simple scanning. Understanding and adopting modern strategies like CTEM and preparing for future threats like quantum decryption are necessary to maintain a strong security posture.
Link: https://novelvista.com/cybersecurity-transformation-trends-2026/

**Data Center Security in a Hybrid World**
As more companies use a mix of on-premise data centers and multiple cloud providers, securing this complex environment has become a major challenge. The increased number of connections and third-party services creates more potential entry points for attackers. Insufficient monitoring across these hybrid environments can create blind spots, preventing companies from seeing and stopping an attack in its early stages.
Why it Matters: Your company's data and applications might be spread across more locations than you realize. A lack of unified security and visibility across all these environments significantly increases the risk of a breach going undetected until it's too late.
Link: https://group-ib.com/blog/data-center-security/
