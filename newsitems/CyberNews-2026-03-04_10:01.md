**Hackers Actively Exploiting High-Severity Memory Flaw**

A high-severity memory corruption vulnerability, identified as CVE-2026-21385, is being actively exploited in the wild. Security researchers report that the sophistication of the ongoing attacks suggests a link to commercial spyware vendors or nation-state hacking groups, who are leveraging the flaw to compromise targets.

Why it Matters: Active exploitation of a memory corruption bug is a serious threat. This type of flaw can often lead to a full system takeover, allowing attackers to steal sensitive enterprise data, deploy ransomware, or establish a persistent foothold in the network. The potential involvement of state-level actors or spyware vendors indicates the flaw is being used in targeted, high-stakes attacks.
