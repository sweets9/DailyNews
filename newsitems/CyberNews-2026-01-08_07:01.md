**CISA Adds Two Actively Exploited Vulnerabilities to KEV Catalog**
The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has added two vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog, indicating they are being actively exploited in the wild. The vulnerabilities are CVE-2009-0556, a code injection flaw in Microsoft Office PowerPoint, and CVE-2025-37164, a code injection vulnerability in HPE OneView.
Why it Matters: Any vulnerability in the KEV catalog should be a top priority for patching. These are not theoretical risks; they are confirmed to be used by attackers. The PowerPoint vulnerability is old, but its inclusion suggests it's being used in recent attacks, possibly targeting legacy systems.
Link: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/01/08/cisa-adds-two-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-kev-catalog

**"MongoBleed" Flaw Under Active Exploitation**
A severe memory disclosure vulnerability in MongoDB, dubbed "MongoBleed" (CVE-2025-14847), is being actively exploited. The flaw allows an unauthenticated remote attacker to read uninitialized memory from the server, potentially exposing sensitive internal data. Over 87,000 servers are estimated to be vulnerable.
Why it Matters: This is a critical vulnerability that can lead to the exposure of sensitive data. The fact that it is being actively exploited means that any internet-facing MongoDB server that is not patched is at high risk of being compromised.
Link: https://www.acronis.com/en-us/blog/posts/cyber-threats-and-trends-report-2026

**Critical "Perfect 10" Vulnerability in SmarterMail**
A critical remote code execution (RCE) vulnerability (CVE-2025-52691) has been discovered in SmarterTools SmarterMail. The Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) has given the flaw a "perfect 10" CVSS score, indicating maximum severity.
Why it Matters: A 10/10 CVSS score means the vulnerability is easy to exploit, requires no user interaction, and can lead to complete system takeover. Organizations using SmarterMail should patch immediately to prevent compromise.

**Covenant Health Ransomware Attack Impact Grows**
A ransomware attack on Covenant Health, first reported in mid-2025, has now been revealed to affect 478,188 patients. The Qilin ransomware group has claimed responsibility, stating they stole 850 GB of data, including Social Security numbers and medical records.
Why it Matters: This is a major breach of sensitive healthcare information. The escalation in the number of affected individuals highlights the long-tail impact of ransomware attacks and the difficulty in containing them.

**European Space Agency (ESA) Data Leaked**
The European Space Agency (ESA) has confirmed a data breach after a hacker advertised 200 GB of stolen data for sale. The data includes source code, API tokens, and credentials from external servers used for unclassified scientific collaboration.
Why it Matters: While the breach appears to be limited to unclassified systems, the stolen source code and credentials could be used to launch further attacks against the ESA or its partners. This incident highlights the security risks of third-party and collaborative environments.
