**Critical Cisco Vulnerability Under Active Attack**
A critical vulnerability, rated 10 out of 10 for severity, has been found in Cisco's Software-Defined Wide-Area Networking (SD-WAN) systems. The American Hospital Association (AHA) has issued a warning that sophisticated hackers are actively exploiting this flaw to gain long-term access to enterprise networks.
**Why it Matters:** A vulnerability this severe and actively exploited poses an immediate and significant risk to any organization using the affected Cisco products. It can lead to a full network compromise, data theft, and persistent unauthorized access.
**Link:** https://www.securityweek.com/rss/

**Massive Breach of French Medical Data Affects 15 Million**
A cyberattack on a medical software provider in France has led to a data breach affecting 15 million people. The breach exposed administrative data and, for some, sensitive doctors' notes. The attack targeted software from Cegedim Sante, used by 1,500 doctors.
**Why it Matters:** This is an extremely serious breach involving highly sensitive and personal health information. It exposes affected individuals to fraud and blackmail, and the organizations involved to severe regulatory fines and reputational damage.
**Link:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/massive-data-breach-hits-15-million-in-france-after-medical-software-hack/

**CarGurus Breach Exposes Data of 12 Million Users**
The automotive marketplace CarGurus suffered a data breach exposing the personal and financial application data of over 12 million users. The hacker group "ShinyHunters" has claimed responsibility, releasing data that includes names, phone numbers, and auto finance application outcomes.
**Why it Matters:** The exposure of financial application data creates a high risk of targeted phishing attacks and identity theft for the millions of users affected. This undermines trust in the platform and exposes the company to legal action.
**Link:** https://haveibeenpwned.com/RecentBreaches

**DIY Retailer ManoMano Breach Impacts 38 Million Customers**
European DIY retail chain ManoMano is notifying 38 million individuals that their data was exposed after a third-party customer service provider was breached. The stolen data includes names, email addresses, and phone numbers.
**Why it Matters:** While no financial data was lost, the sheer scale of this breach provides a massive amount of data for cybercriminals to conduct large-scale phishing and spam campaigns.
**Link:** https://www.scmagazine.com/brief/manomano-data-breach-impacts-38-million-customers-via-third-party-provider

**Critical Flaws Found in Anthropic's AI Coding Assistant**
Security researchers discovered three critical vulnerabilities in Anthropic's Claude Code, an AI assistant for developers. The flaws could have allowed an attacker to take over a developer's coding session, execute commands, and steal secret API keys by tricking the user into loading a malicious file. Anthropic has patched the issues.
**Why it Matters:** This highlights the emerging security risks in AI-powered development tools, which could lead to software supply chain attacks where malicious code is inserted into a company's products without their knowledge.
**Link:** https://www.databreachtoday.com/malicious-repository-files-could-hijack-claude-code-sessions-a-24430

**DJI Robot Vacuums Exposed Sensitive User Data**
A server vulnerability in DJI's robot vacuums allowed for unauthorized access to sensitive data, including home floor plans and potentially live video feeds from the devices.
**Why it Matters:** This is a serious privacy breach that exposes the intimate details of a person's home. It underscores the security risks associated with internet-connected (IoT) devices in both home and business environments.
**Link:** https://www.scmagazine.com/brief/dji-robot-vacuums-exposed-sensitive-data-server-vulnerability
