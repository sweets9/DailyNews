**Critical SmarterMail Vulnerability Actively Exploited**
A critical vulnerability in SmarterMail (CVE-2026-23760) is being actively exploited, according to an alert from CISA. The flaw allows an unauthenticated attacker to reset the password of any user, including system administrators, by sending a specially crafted request. This can lead to a full compromise of the mail server, data breaches, and further network intrusion. CISA has added this vulnerability to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog, requiring federal agencies to patch immediately. A similar critical remote code execution flaw in Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE) OneView was also highlighted.
Why it Matters: An attacker can gain complete control over a company's email system, which is a treasure trove of sensitive data and a launchpad for other attacks. The "actively exploited" status means this is an immediate and urgent threat. Any organization using SmarterMail should patch immediately.
Link: https://www.cisa.gov/known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog

**Vulnerability Exploits Surpass Phishing as Top Attack Vector**
Exploiting software vulnerabilities is now the most common way attackers breach corporate networks, accounting for nearly 40% of all intrusions in the last quarter of 2025, according to a new report from Cisco Talos. This marks a significant shift, pushing phishing into second place. The report highlights the incredible speed at which attackers can "weaponize" a newly announced flaw, with functional exploits appearing in as little as 30 hours.
Why it Matters: This trend change requires a strategic shift in defense. While employee training on phishing remains important, the emphasis must now be on rapid, prioritized vulnerability management and patching. The window for defenders to patch a vulnerability before it's exploited is shrinking dramatically.
Link: https://gopher.security/cybersecurity-news-top-exploits-and-intrusions-in-focus/

**Security Researchers Warn of "Vibe Crime," AI-Powered Attacks**
A new threat model called "vibe crime" is emerging, where attackers use autonomous AI systems to plan, execute, and optimize cyberattacks with minimal human oversight. Researchers predict a move from "Cybercrime-as-a-Service" (where humans rent tools) to "Cybercrime-as-a-Servant," where a purchased AI agent continuously runs and adapts attack campaigns to achieve a goal, such as stealing specific data.
Why it Matters: This represents a significant escalation in the sophistication of automated attacks. If successful, this would allow adversaries to launch more complex, adaptive, and persistent campaigns at a scale that is impossible to defend against with traditional, static security controls.
Link: https://coesecurity.com/vibe-crime-is-here/

**"Shadow AI" Usage Increases Enterprise Data Breach Costs**
Employees using personal or unauthorized Generative AI (GenAI) accounts for work-related tasks are creating a significant security blind spot known as "Shadow AI." When data breaches involve this unsanctioned AI use, the average cost of the incident increases by over $670,000, according to new industry analysis. Sensitive corporate data entered into these public AI models can be inadvertently leaked or used to train the model, bypassing all enterprise security and data loss prevention controls.
Why it Matters: Businesses may be leaking their most sensitive data and intellectual property through tools they don't even know are being used. This highlights an urgent need for clear corporate policies on AI usage and tools to monitor for unauthorized data flows to public AI platforms.
Link: https://www.vectra.ai/blogpost/the-real-financial-impact-of-shadow-ai-in-the-enterprise
