**Critical Cisco Flaw Under Active Exploit**
A critical vulnerability, identified as CVE-2026-20045, in Cisco's Unified Communications Manager is being actively exploited by attackers. The flaw allows for remote code execution without authentication, enabling attackers to take full control of affected systems. Cisco has released security patches and urges administrators to apply them immediately. The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has added the vulnerability to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog.
Why it Matters: Unified Communications systems are central to enterprise communication, handling voice, video, and messaging. A full takeover of these systems can lead to espionage, service disruption, and further network intrusion. The active exploitation means this is an immediate and ongoing threat.
Link: https://www.securityweek.com/cisa-warns-of-attackers-exploiting-cisco-unified-cm-zero-day/

**Attackers Target FortiGate Firewalls Bypassing SSO**
A new wave of attacks is targeting FortiGate firewalls. Attackers are able to bypass FortiCloud single sign-on (SSO) authentication. This allows them to create new user accounts on the devices and alter security configurations, potentially leaving the network exposed.
Why it Matters: Firewalls are the primary security barrier for most organizations. Bypassing authentication on a firewall is a severe security failure, as it allows an attacker to control the flow of traffic in and out of the network, disable security rules, and hide their malicious activity.
Link: https://www.securityweek.com/new-wave-of-attacks-target-fortigate-firewalls/

**Critical Flaw in WordPress Plugin Allows Full Site Takeover**
A critical vulnerability (CVE-2026-23550) with a severity score of 10.0 has been discovered in the WordPress 'Modular DS' plugin. The flaw allows an unauthenticated attacker to gain administrative privileges, leading to a full takeover of the website. Attackers can use this access to deploy malware, steal data, or deface the site. Users are advised to update to version 2.5.2 immediately or disable the plugin.
Why it Matters: WordPress powers a significant portion of the web. A vulnerability this severe in a plugin can expose thousands of websites to immediate compromise, affecting businesses that rely on their web presence for commerce, lead generation, and customer communication.
Link: https://thehackernews.com/2026/01/critical-wordpress-plugin-flaw-allows.html

**Oracle Issues Massive Patch Update for 337 Vulnerabilities**
Oracle has released its first quarterly Critical Patch Update (CPU) for 2026, addressing 337 security flaws across a wide range of its products. The update includes patches for its database, middleware, and various enterprise applications. Several of the vulnerabilities could be exploited remotely without authentication.
Why it Matters: Oracle products are deeply embedded in many large enterprises. The large number of vulnerabilities highlights the vast attack surface these products represent. Failing to apply these patches can leave critical business systems, including sensitive databases, exposed to attack.
Link: https://www.securityweek.com/oracle-releases-january-2026-critical-patch-update/

**SmarterMail Flaw Allows Admin Account Hijack**
A serious authentication bypass vulnerability (WT-2026-0001) was patched in the SmarterMail email server. The flaw allowed an attacker to reset the password for any account, including the system administrator, by sending a specially crafted web request. This could lead to a full compromise of the email server.
Why it Matters: Email servers contain a company's most sensitive communications. An attacker with administrative control over the email server can read, send, and delete emails, create fake accounts, and use the server to launch further phishing attacks against employees or customers.
Link: https://thehackernews.com/2026/01/smartermail-auth-bypass-flaw.html

**Report: 77% of Businesses Suffer From Insider-Related Data Loss**
A recent study found that over three-quarters of enterprises have experienced data loss stemming from an insider threat within the last 18 months. The report clarifies that most of these incidents are due to employee mistakes and negligence rather than malicious intent. This highlights a significant gap in data loss prevention (DLP) strategies.
Why it Matters: While external threats get more headlines, this shows that the biggest risk to data is often internal. Businesses must implement better training and technical controls to prevent accidental data exposure, which can be just as costly as a malicious breach in terms of fines, reputational damage, and loss of intellectual property.
Link: https://www.blackfog.com/confronting-the-reality-of-enterprise-data-loss/
