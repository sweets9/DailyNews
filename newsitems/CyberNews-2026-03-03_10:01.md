**CISA Warns of Actively Exploited "Resurge" Malware**
The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has issued an urgent advisory about a new malware family called "Resurge." This malware is actively exploiting multiple zero-day vulnerabilities, which are flaws in software that are unknown to the vendor. The malware is modular and evasive, allowing it to gain initial access, remain hidden, and spread through compromised networks.
Why it Matters: A zero-day vulnerability that is being actively exploited is a critical threat. Businesses need to be aware of this malware and follow CISA's recommendations, which include prioritizing patching, using behavioral and AI-assisted detection, and implementing the principle of least privilege.
Link: https://coesecurity.com/cisa-warns-of-resurge-malware-exploiting-zero-days/

**Credential Compromise Remains the Top Attack Vector**
Multiple reports highlight that stolen credentials are the most common way attackers breach enterprise networks. Rather than relying on complex zero-day exploits, attackers are using credentials obtained from infostealer malware or through weak help desk verification processes to bypass multi-factor authentication (MFA).
Why it Matters: This trend shows that even with advanced security measures like MFA, determined attackers can gain access if they have valid credentials. It underscores the importance of treating identity as a continuous risk and monitoring for suspicious activity, rather than just relying on a one-time authentication.
Link: https://iddataweb.com/credential-compromise-is-the-leading-attack-vector-why-enterprise-security-must-shift-to-identity-threat-detection/

**Supply Chain Attacks Now the Most Prevalent Cyber Threat**
Nearly one-third of businesses were affected by supply chain attacks in the last year. Large enterprises are particularly vulnerable due to their extensive network of suppliers, which expands the potential attack surface. Attackers are exploiting these trusted relationships to gain access to their ultimate targets.
Why it Matters: A supply chain attack can be devastating because it can be difficult to detect and can come from a trusted source. Businesses need to vet their suppliers' security and have a plan in place to mitigate the risk of a supply chain compromise.
Link: https://www.kaspersky.com/blog/supply-chain-attacks-in-2025/

**AI is the Top Data Security Risk, Says Thales Report**
A new report from Thales reveals that 70% of organizations see Artificial Intelligence (AI) as their top data security risk. The report also found that 47% of sensitive data in the cloud is not encrypted. AI systems often have broad access to corporate data, and if not properly secured, they can amplify existing vulnerabilities.
Why it Matters: As businesses increasingly adopt AI, they are creating a new "insider threat." If an AI system is compromised, it could lead to a massive data breach. This highlights the urgent need for better data governance and encryption, especially in cloud environments.
Link: https://www.channelinsider.com/es/security/ai-top-data-security-risk/

**Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT) is Crucial for Enterprise Security**
Multiple security experts are emphasizing the importance of VAPT as a core component of any enterprise security strategy. VAPT is the process of identifying, quantifying, and prioritizing the vulnerabilities in a system. It helps organizations understand their real-world risk exposure and take steps to mitigate it before attackers can exploit any weaknesses.
Why it Matters: In the current threat landscape, a proactive approach to security is essential. VAPT allows businesses to find and fix security holes before they can be exploited, reducing the risk of a successful attack.
