**Critical Vulnerability in ServiceNow Platform Exposes Enterprise Data**
A critical vulnerability has been discovered in ServiceNow's platform that could expose sensitive customer data and grant administrative access to attackers. The flaw resides in a legacy chatbot component and is exacerbated by the integration of modern AI features. The vulnerability stems from weak authentication in the chatbot, which could be exploited to gain unauthorized access to enterprise systems.
Why it Matters: ServiceNow is a widely used platform for IT service management and business operations. A critical vulnerability could allow attackers to access and exfiltrate sensitive business data, disrupt operations, and gain a foothold for further attacks within a corporate network. The risk is particularly high for organizations that have integrated the chatbot with other critical systems.

**Critical Flaw in n8n Workflow Automation Tool Allows Credential Theft**
A critical vulnerability, with a severity score of 10 out of 10, has been found in the n8n workflow automation platform. This flaw could allow an attacker to bypass security measures and access sensitive credentials and other secrets stored within the platform. The vulnerability is described as a "content-type confusion" bug, which can trick the application into mishandling data.
Why it Matters: Workflow automation tools like n8n are often connected to many different applications and services, handling sensitive data and API keys. A vulnerability that exposes credentials can lead to a cascade of security breaches across multiple systems, as attackers use the stolen secrets to move laterally and compromise other parts of the infrastructure.

**"CodeBreach" Vulnerability in AWS Console Poses Supply Chain Risk**
A critical vulnerability named "CodeBreach" has been identified in the AWS Console. This flaw could allow an unauthenticated attacker to compromise software build environments and hijack code repositories, posing a significant threat to the software supply chain. An attacker could potentially inject malicious code into an organization's software without their knowledge.
Why it Matters: The software supply chain is a prime target for attackers. By compromising a single piece of software, they can distribute malware to thousands of downstream users. A vulnerability in a major cloud provider like AWS that affects code repositories and build environments is a serious threat to any organization that develops and deploys software.

**Artificial Intelligence: A Double-Edged Sword for Enterprise Security**
Recent reports highlight the dual role of AI in cybersecurity. Attackers are increasingly using generative AI to create more sophisticated and convincing phishing emails and social engineering attacks at scale, drastically reducing the time required to launch effective campaigns. Conversely, AI-powered defensive tools are helping organizations to detect and respond to breaches faster, contributing to a decline in the average cost of a data breach for the first time in years.
Why it Matters: The rise of AI in cyberattacks means that traditional security measures may no longer be sufficient. Phishing and social engineering are already a leading cause of breaches, and AI will only make them more effective. Businesses need to be aware of this evolving threat and invest in AI-powered defenses to keep pace.

**Enterprises Shift to Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM)**
In response to the rapidly evolving threat landscape, organizations are moving away from traditional periodic security assessments (like annual penetration tests) and toward a model of Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM). This approach focuses on providing ongoing visibility into an organization's attack surface and prioritizing vulnerabilities that are genuinely exploitable and pose a material risk to the business.
Why it Matters: Attackers are exploiting known vulnerabilities faster than ever. The traditional model of periodic scanning and patching is no longer adequate to protect against agile adversaries. CTEM allows organizations to take a more proactive and risk-based approach to vulnerability management, focusing their limited resources on the threats that matter most.
Link: https://www.teiss.co.uk/the-next-shift-in-enterprise-cyber-risk/
